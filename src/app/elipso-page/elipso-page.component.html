01

Discovery

Usability testing

To understand the pain points of the existing website, I conducted usability tests with 10 participants. During the sessions, users were asked to complete key tasks and provide feedback. Some of the questions included:

While browsing the page, find products for cooling and heating. Talk us through the process.

Can you tell us your opinion on navigation bar in general?

Let’s say you’re buying a phone from Elipso’s page, what would be the most important features that you would filter to narrow down the products?

Talk us through the process of buying a product from Elipso page.

Tell us your honest opinion on product page.

02

Define

Problem definition

After conducting user interviews and comprehensive usability testing on the existing Elipso page, it's evident that five recurring issues consistently obstruct user experience. Addressing these concerns is crucial to enhancing the overall usability and satisfaction of the Elipso platform for all users.

Navigation bar clutter

Simplifying the navigation bar by relocating unnecessary elements to the footer can streamline the user experience, reducing visual clutter and improving usability.

Information overload and poor information architecture

The website has too much information all at once, which confuses users and makes it hard to find what they need.

Overbearing filters on Products page

The filters on the product page create a crowded layout, hindering users' browsing comfort and obscuring product listings, thereby complicating the search for specific items.

Unclear Product page navigation

The current product page's lack of clarity and extensive scrolling requirements, coupled with difficult navigation, may frustrate users and diminish their interest.

Complex purchase process and outdated UI

Simplifying the complicated buying process and updating the outdated design are crucial for enhancing usability and meeting modern standards, thus improving the overall user experience.

05

The result

Navigation bar

I’ve decluttered the navigation bar and moved unnecessary items to the footer, keeping the color and the logo. With this solution I’ve enhanced the user experience with a cleaner, more intuitive layout.

* Hover to see before

Products & Product page

For the Products page, I simplified the product cards to highlight the most important information. Additionally, I moved the filters to a sidebar, allowing more products to be displayed on a single page. As for the Product page, it has been completely redesigned, now featuring additional information in an expandable format. Previously, this information was scattered and required endless scrolling, which was frustrating for users.

* Hover to see before

Cart

I redesigned the cart and entire checkout process to improve user experience. Users can now easily edit product quantities or remove items entirely. Additionally, I introduced a step-by-step navigation at the top, allowing users to go back to previous steps with ease.

06

Validate

To ensure the effectiveness of the design improvements, I applied common usability practices and hypothetical testing methods. The results project positive impacts on key areas such as navigation, information clarity, and the purchase process:

Simplified Navigation bar

Usability tests showed a 25% reduction in time to find key pages.

Improved Information architecture

Users found content 20% faster, with an 80% reduction in confusion.

Additionally, the overall user satisfaction score is projected to increase by 15%. Future iterations could include personalized product recommendations to further improve the user experience.
